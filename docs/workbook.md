# Your Money Mind Workbook
## Practical Exercises to Transform Your Financial Life

### How to Use This Workbook

This isn't a book you read once and put on a shelf. It's a working document where you'll:
- Discover your patterns
- Try new approaches
- Track your progress
- Build new habits

**Guidelines**:
- Write in it. Make it messy. Cross things out.
- Start with exercises that call to you
- You don't have to do them in order
- Come back and repeat exercises as you grow
- Be honest. No one sees this but you.

### Exercise 1.4: Identify Your Money Parts

**Time**: 30 minutes

**Instructions**: 
Read each description. Put a checkmark next to parts that sound like you. You'll likely have several.

**The Protector Parts** (trying to keep you safe):

□ **The Worrier**
- Constantly scanning for financial danger
- "What if we run out of money?"
- Checks accounts multiple times per day
- Has trouble enjoying what you have

□ **The Saver/Hoarder**  
- Feels anxious spending even on needs
- "We might need this someday"
- Has trouble letting money go
- Saves excessively even when safe

□ **The Avoider**
- Doesn't want to look at bank accounts
- "If I ignore it, it's not real"
- Procrastinates on financial tasks
- Gets physically uncomfortable with money talk

□ **The Inner Critic**
- Harsh voice judging financial decisions
- "You're so stupid with money"
- Focuses on every mistake
- Makes you feel ashamed

□ **The Controller**
- Must track every penny
- "I need perfect systems"
- Exhausting perfectionism
- Anxious if anything is out of place

**The Reactive Parts** (respond when protectors fail):

□ **The Spender**
- Shops when stressed or sad
- "I deserve this, I've had a hard day"
- Retail therapy is real therapy
- Creates debt then feels guilty

□ **The Risk-Taker**
- Makes impulsive financial decisions
- "This can't lose!"
- Chases get-rich-quick schemes
- Gambles or makes risky investments

**The Wounded Parts** (carrying old pain):

□ **The Scared Kid**
- Remembers financial insecurity from childhood
- "We'll never have enough"
- Feels small and powerless with money
- Needs reassurance

□ **The Ashamed One**
- Feels fundamentally broken about money
- "I'm bad with money because I'm bad"
- Hides financial reality from everyone
- Deep shame about money mistakes
