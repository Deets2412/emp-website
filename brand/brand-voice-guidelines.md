# The Emotional Money Podcast — Brand Voice Guidelines

> *Version 1.0 | March 2026*

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**The Emotional Money Podcast (EMP)** explores the emotional side of money — why we feel the way we do about it, and how understanding those feelings transforms our financial lives.

**Core belief:** Your financial life is shaped by your emotions first and your strategies second. When you understand the feelings driving your decisions, lasting change becomes possible.

**Philosophical framework — "Feeling Before Fixing":**
EMP is grounded in affective liminal psychology — the idea that real transformation happens when we sit with our emotions rather than rushing to fix them. Like Annie Grace's "This Naked Mind" approach to alcohol, EMP doesn't lecture people about what they *should* do with money. Instead, it helps them understand *why* they do what they do — and through that awareness, behaviour shifts naturally.

This means EMP never prescribes. It illuminates. The audience arrives at their own insights because they've been given the space and tools to feel, reflect, and evolve.

## 4. Two Host Voices

EMP has one unified brand voice but two distinct host personalities. Both voices live within the brand — they complement and contrast each other, creating the dynamic that makes EMP unique.

### David — The Experienced Guide

**Archetype:** The wise friend who's seen it all but never talks down to you.

- **Tone:** Warm, reflective, grounded. Speaks from decades of experience in wealth management but leads with emotional curiosity, not authority.
- **Strength:** He makes complex financial and psychological ideas feel simple and human. He's the one who says, "I've sat across the table from hundreds of people, and here's what I've noticed..."
- **Language style:** Thoughtful, measured, occasionally poetic. Uses stories and observations from real life. Comfortable with pauses and silence.
- **Watch out for:** Sounding preachy or lecturing. David's power comes from sharing, not telling.

**Example David line:**
*"I spent 20 years thinking I was helping people with their money. Turns out I was helping them with their feelings — I just didn't know it yet."*

### Emme — The Fresh Lens

**Archetype:** The curious younger voice who asks the questions everyone's thinking but nobody says out loud.

- **Tone:** Direct, warm, unfiltered. Brings Gen Z honesty and psychological insight. Not afraid to challenge assumptions — including David's.
- **Strength:** She makes the audience feel permission to be confused, overwhelmed, or behind. She normalises the struggle of figuring money out.
- **Language style:** Conversational, sharp, occasionally cheeky. Uses contemporary references naturally (not forced). Comfortable calling things out.
- **Watch out for:** Sounding too casual or flippant about serious topics. Emme's edge is always paired with genuine care.

**Example Emme line:**
*"Can we just admit that nobody actually reads their super statement? Like, I know I should care. But the feeling I get when I open it is literally dread. So I don't. And I don't think I'm the only one."*

### When They're Together

The magic of EMP is in the dynamic — David brings depth, Emme brings directness. They model what healthy intergenerational money conversations look like: respectful disagreement, genuine curiosity about each other's perspective, and the willingness to say "I don't know."

## 5. Tone-by-Context Matrix

The core voice stays consistent, but the tone shifts depending on the context:

| Context | Tone | Energy | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Podcast episodes** | Conversational, exploratory, intimate | Medium-high — like a deep late-night talk | "What if the reason you can't stick to a budget has nothing to do with discipline?" |
| **Social media (Instagram/TikTok)** | Punchy, relatable, scroll-stopping | High — hook fast, resonate deep | "Your spending habits aren't random. They're a map of your feelings." |
| **Newsletter** | Warm, reflective, personal | Medium — like a letter from a thoughtful friend | "This week, something happened that made me rethink everything I thought about financial anxiety..." |
| **Courses & programs** | Supportive, structured, encouraging | Calm-steady — like a guide walking beside you | "In this module, we'll explore the emotion underneath your most common money pattern." |
| **Website copy** | Inviting, clear, aspirational | Medium — confident but not salesy | "Your financial life isn't just about numbers — it's about the emotions driving every decision you make." |
| **YouTube descriptions** | Clear, keyword-aware, warm | Low-medium — informative but still on-brand | "In this episode, David and Emme explore why heartbreak hits your wallet..." |
| **Coaching/events** | Deeply personal, held, safe | Low-calm — creating space for vulnerability | "There's no wrong answer here. We're just noticing what comes up." |
| **Books** | Authoritative but intimate, research-backed but accessible | Measured — considered, layered, narrative-driven | "The money story you tell yourself was written before you could spell your own name." |

## 6. Language Guidelines

### Words & Phrases We Use

- Money story / money narrative
- Emotional relationship with money
- Money patterns
- Feeling before fixing
- Financial emotions / emotional finances
- "What comes up for you when..."
- "Notice how..."
- "Sit with that for a moment"
- Awareness, insight, shift, transform
- Real, honest, human, messy

### Words & Phrases We Avoid

- Financial freedom / financial independence (too hustle-culture)
- Money mindset (overused, oversimplified)
- Hack, trick, secret (too clickbaity)
- Should, must, need to (too prescriptive)
- Passive income, side hustle, grind (wrong energy)
- Clinical terms without explanation (attachment theory, cognitive dissonance — unless unpacked in plain language)
- "Just" (minimises feelings — "just save more," "just budget better")
- Wealthy / rich as aspirational identity labels

### Psychological Language — The EMP Way

EMP is rooted in psychology but never sounds like a textbook. The rule: **use the concept, not the jargon.**

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---|---|
| "Cognitive behavioural reframing" | "Changing the story you tell yourself" |
| "Affective regulation" | "Learning to sit with uncomfortable money feelings" |
| "Attachment style" | "The way you learned to relate to security" |
| "Liminal space" | "That in-between place where the old way doesn't work but the new way hasn't landed yet" |
| "Neuroplasticity" | "Your brain can actually rewire how you feel about money" |

## 7. Content Pillars

These four pillars guide all content. Every piece of EMP content should connect to at least one:

1. **Emotional Deep Dives** — Exploring the feelings that shape your money story (fear, shame, joy, guilt, hope)
2. **Money Personalities** — Understanding your unique emotional wiring around money (the five Money Personas as a content hook)
3. **Generations & Culture** — How your background, generation, and upbringing shape your money emotions
4. **Real Life Money** — Navigating everyday emotional money moments (breakups, windfalls, career changes, family dynamics)

The five Money Personas are a content and social media hook — a relatable entry point into the deeper work. They are not the brand itself, but they're a powerful tool for engagement and self-discovery.

- 🌿 **Balanced Builder** — Steady, grounded, growing
- 🛡️ **Anxious Protector** — Driven by the need for security
- 🦋 **Avoidant Free Spirit** — Living in the moment, avoiding the numbers
- ⚔️ **Wounded Warrior** — Carrying past financial pain forward
- 🔍 **Perfectionist Analyzer** — Seeking the perfect move before any move

**Usage guidelines:** The personas should feel inviting and non-judgmental. They're mirrors, not labels. Nobody is "stuck" in one persona — the point is awareness and growth.

## 9. Brand Voice Dos and Don'ts

### Do

- Lead with emotion, follow with insight
- Ask questions more than you give answers
- Use real stories (anonymised when needed) to illustrate ideas
- Acknowledge that money is hard, complicated, and deeply personal
- Make people feel seen and less alone
- Bridge generations — respect both David's experience and Emme's perspective
- Use Australian English spelling and references naturally
- Embrace silence and space — not every moment needs to be filled

### Don't

- Prescribe financial strategies or specific advice
- Use fear or scarcity to drive engagement
- Shame anyone for their money situation or decisions
- Oversimplify complex emotions ("just think positive!")
- Sound like a financial advisor giving compliance-approved copy
- Use forced Gen Z slang or try-hard youth language
- Talk about money in purely numerical or transactional terms
- Promise outcomes ("you'll be financially free in 90 days")

## 11. Brand Promise

**To our audience:** We will never make you feel broken for how you feel about money. We will help you understand those feelings, and through that understanding, transform your financial life from the inside out.

**To each other:** We show up as real humans — not polished experts. We model the kind of open, honest, intergenerational money conversation we want to see in the world.

---

## 12. Quick Reference — Voice Check

Before publishing any EMP content, ask:

1. **Does it lead with feeling?** (Not strategy, not data, not advice)
2. **Would someone feel seen, not judged?**
3. **Is it a mirror, not a lecture?**
4. **Does it sound like a real human said it?**
5. **Would both David and Emme be proud of it?**

If yes to all five — ship it.
