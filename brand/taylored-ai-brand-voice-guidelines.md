# Taylored AI — Brand Voice Guidelines

> *Version 1.0 | March 2026*

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**Taylored AI** is a bespoke AI solutions and education brand co-founded by David Taylor (DT) and Lachlan Clark (LC) — combining David's self-taught AI building skills with Lachlan's deep media, marketing, and advertising expertise.

**Core belief:** AI isn't magic and it isn't hype — it's a practical tool that anyone can learn to wield. The gap isn't intelligence or technical skill. It's just knowing where to start and having the right people show you the way.

**Origin story:** David came from finance, taught himself AI by building — not studying — and rapidly shipped a portfolio of real products across marketing, finance, crypto, and podcasting. Lachlan came from media — breakfast radio, media sales, content strategy, and heading up advertising for Queensland. Together, they combine the builder and the storyteller: David makes the thing, Lachlan makes sure people hear about it. Taylored AI exists because they believe AI should be accessible to everyone — and if you'd rather someone do it for you, they'll build it.

**The name:** "Taylored" is a deliberate play — David Taylor's surname meets the promise of bespoke, custom-fit AI solutions. Nothing off-the-shelf. Everything tailored to the specific problem, business, or person.

## 4. Two Co-Founder Voices

Taylored AI has one unified brand voice but two distinct co-founder personalities. David builds, Lachlan amplifies. Together they cover the full spectrum — from making the product to making it matter.

### David Taylor (DT) — The Builder

**Archetype:** The practical neighbour who happens to be really good at AI — and genuinely wants to help you figure it out too.

- **Tone:** Conversational, warm, no-nonsense. Like explaining something to a smart friend over coffee — not presenting at a conference.
- **Strength:** Credibility through doing, not credentials. David doesn't say "AI can transform your business." He says "Here's the tool I built last week that does exactly this. Let me show you."
- **Language style:** Plain language, short sentences, concrete examples. Uses "we built" more than "you should." Shows rather than tells.
- **Humility lever:** Comfortable being early-stage. Doesn't pretend to be bigger than he is — and that honesty is actually a superpower.

**Example David lines:**

*"Two months ago I'd never built an AI app. Now I've shipped six. Here's what I actually learned — not the LinkedIn version."*

*"I'm not going to tell you AI will revolutionise your business. I'm going to show you three things it can do for you this week."*

*"I taught myself this stuff. If I can do it, you definitely can. And if you'd rather not, we'll build it for you."*

### Lachlan Clark (LC) — The Amplifier

**Archetype:** The media-savvy operator who knows how to make great work impossible to ignore.

- **Tone:** Energetic, sharp, commercially aware. Speaks the language of audiences, reach, and impact. Brings the broadcast instinct — he knows what makes people stop and pay attention.
- **Strength:** Lachlan bridges the gap between what's been built and who needs to hear about it. His media and advertising background means he thinks in audiences, channels, and stories — not just features.
- **Language style:** Confident, punchy, audience-first. Naturally thinks about the hook, the angle, the "why should anyone care." More polished than David's builder voice, but still grounded and real.
- **Watch out for:** Sounding too "adland" or polished — Taylored AI is anti-hype, so Lachlan's media instincts need to stay anchored in the brand's honesty.

**Example Lachlan lines:**

*"David builds it. I make sure the right people know about it. That's the whole model."*

*"I spent years selling airtime and ad space. Now I'm helping businesses understand that AI isn't a future thing — it's a right now thing."*

*"The best product in the world doesn't matter if nobody knows it exists. That's where I come in."*

### When They're Together

The dynamic is builder meets broadcaster. David shows what's possible, Lachlan makes it resonate. David grounds the brand in technical credibility, Lachlan drives commercial momentum. They keep each other honest — David stops Lachlan from over-promising, Lachlan stops David from under-selling.

## 6. Language Guidelines

### Words & Phrases We Use

- Built, shipped, made, created
- Custom, bespoke, tailored, fit-for-purpose
- Practical, real, working, actual
- "Here's what I built"
- "Let me show you"
- "If I can do it..."
- Self-taught, learned by doing
- Tool, solution, workflow, system
- Plain English, clear, accessible
- Honest, real talk, no BS

### Words & Phrases We Avoid

- Revolutionise, disrupt, transform (too hype-y)
- Cutting-edge, next-gen, state-of-the-art (buzzwords)
- AI-powered everything (overused and meaningless)
- Leverage, synergise, optimise (corporate speak)
- 10x, scale, growth hack (bro culture)
- "Thought leader" or "expert" (let others call us that)
- Guaranteed results, ROI promises (overselling)
- "Simple" or "easy" (dismisses the learning curve)
- Unlock, supercharge, turbocharge (inflated language)

### The Anti-Hype Translation Table

| Hype version | Taylored AI version |
|---|---|
| "AI will revolutionise your business" | "Here are three things AI can actually do for you right now" |
| "Our cutting-edge AI solutions" | "I'll build exactly what you need — nothing more, nothing less" |
| "Leverage AI to 10x your productivity" | "This tool saves me about 3 hours a week. Want one?" |
| "I'm an AI expert" | "I've built a lot of things with AI. I'm still learning every day." |
| "Transform your workflow with AI" | "Let me show you what I made. If it's useful, I can build something similar for you." |
| "Schedule a free strategy call" | "Want to chat about what you're trying to solve? Happy to help." |

## 7. Content Pillars

Every piece of Taylored AI content should connect to at least one of these:

1. **Build Log** — What David and Lachlan are actually building, shipping, and learning. Real projects, real challenges, real outcomes. The behind-the-scenes of an AI business in motion.

2. **AI Made Practical** — Demystifying AI for non-technical people. Breaking down what's real vs. hype. Showing how normal businesses can use AI today (not in some theoretical future).

3. **The Self-Taught Path** — David's journey from finance to AI builder. Lessons, mistakes, breakthroughs. Encouraging others that they can learn this too.

4. **Go-To-Market & Growth** — Lachlan's lens on how to position, launch, and grow AI products. Media strategy, audience building, and commercial thinking for the AI age.

5. **Client Stories** — What was built, why, and what happened. Honest case studies that include what worked and what didn't (when permission allows).

## 9. Brand Voice Dos and Don'ts

### Do

- Lead with what you've built — demos over decks, always
- Be honest about your journey — self-taught is a strength, not a weakness
- Show the messy middle — people connect with the real process
- Use plain language that anyone can understand
- Be generous with knowledge — teach freely, charge for implementation
- Acknowledge what you don't know — it builds trust
- Let the portfolio do the heavy lifting
- Stay humble — the brand is early and that's OK

### Don't

- Hype AI capabilities beyond what's real
- Use jargon to sound impressive
- Pretend to be bigger than you are
- Promise specific ROI or transformation outcomes
- Copy the "AI influencer" playbook (overproduced, overpromised)
- Talk about AI in the abstract — always ground it in something you've actually done
- Knock other people's approaches — just focus on yours
- Rush to scale the brand before the work proves itself

## 13. Quick Reference — Voice Check

Before publishing any Taylored AI content, ask:

1. **Does it show something real?** (Not theoretical, not aspirational — actual)
2. **Would a non-technical person understand it?**
3. **Is it honest about limitations?**
4. **Does it sound like David and Lachlan, not a marketing department?**
5. **Is it anti-hype?**

If yes to all five — ship it.
